About Us

Taurus Civils (Pty) Ltd is a Zimbabwean-owned construction & civil engineering company established in 2015. Registered with CIFOZ & ZBCA in 2019. We specialize in building construction, civil works, road infrastructure, stormwater management, and project management.

“Taurus remains an equal opportunity employer. Our business’ biggest currency as a company is quality: delivering projects on time, within budget, and safely with minimal impact on the environment. Through quality works, we continue to secure repeat business, further harnessing and enhancing our relationships with our partners, giving us greater opportunities to explore new horizons.”

Taurus Civils is a building and infrastructure construction company specializing in heavy duty concrete works, brickworks, and wet-trades. Registered in 2019, the company was already active in the construction space prior to registration. Our genuine desire is to contribute positively to the development of the construction industry. Currently, the company employs 65 people directly and 27 through various specialized trade sub-contractors.

“As Taurus, we continuously strive to be the best in what we do and therefore deliberately seek new and better ways of delivering a quality product to our clients on time, without compromise. We aim to become a hub of thought-leaders whose voice must be heard in the infrastructure space within Zimbabwe. This realization has driven our sourcing of highly skilled and experienced individuals, strategic relationships with key players in the industry, and our resolve to make research and development part of our continuous learning culture going forward.”

Our core focus within the infrastructure and construction value chain:

  • Building and masonry works
  • Civil Construction
  • Roads and bulk earthworks
  • Major storm water management
  • Water reservoirs, wastewater treatment plants, and heavy concrete works
  • Bridges and other related structures

“The quality of our works in the first phase of the Beitbridge Border project enabled us to secure repeat works in Zimbabwe. This project is extremely important as it improves ease of access into and out of Zimbabwe and beyond, driving economic activity that reduces unemployment and contributes positively to socio-economic development.

The beauty of our industry is that we create legacy, monuments, and landmarks that will be there for generations to see and benefit from. We believe such capital projects are part of our autobiographical footprint that we will always be proud of.”
